# TLG Andon — Vision Board

> *"The first thing I open every morning."*

---

## North Star

Andon is not a construction management platform.

Andon is **the Decision Layer for Field Operations.**

Construction is where we prove it works. The architecture applies anywhere uncertainty needs to be reduced in real-time.

---

## The Core Philosophy

> *"The product's job is to reduce uncertainty, not store information."*

Information storage has become a commodity. Reducing uncertainty is much harder — and much more valuable.

Every feature, every integration, every decision should be measured against this question:

**Does this reduce uncertainty for the person in the field?**

If not, don't build it.

---

## Pricing Vision (Value-Based)

Not launch recommendations — examples of value-based thinking.

| Tier | Homes/Month | Monthly Price |
|---|---|---|
| **Small Builder** | 5–10 | $99–$149 |
| **Mid Builder** | 10–30 | $249–$499 |
| **Larger Builder** | 30–100 | $750–$1,500 |

A nice dashboard can't command these prices.

A product that becomes **part of how the superintendent runs the day** — that avoids costly mistakes and increases capacity — changes the conversation entirely.

## Strategic Position: The Operations Layer

### Don't compete. Enhance.

```
Buildertrend / JobTread / CoConstruct / Procore
           ↓
        Schedule
           ↓
    Andon watches (reads schedule)
           ↓
    Sub sends SMS / email / voice
           ↓
    AI classifies & creates card
           ↓
    Andon writes back to Buildertrend
           ↓
    Builder never leaves Buildertrend
           ↓
    Superintendent lives in Andon
```

This is a fundamentally better position than building Yet Another Construction Platform.

### The Architecture

```
Buildertrend · JobTread · CoConstruct · Procore
Excel · Google Calendar · Email · SMS · Photos · Voice
           ↓
    ┌─────────────────────┐
    │   Andon Decision    │
    │   Engine            │
    └─────────────────────┘
           ↓
      Superintendent
```

Everything feeds in. Everything reads out.

### The Moat

- Not AI (everyone has AI)
- Not SMS (easy to copy)
- Not the dashboard (commodity)

The moat is the **Decision Engine** — the rules, the classifier, the escalation logic, the uncertainty-reduction layer that sits on top of whatever tools the builder already uses. That layer gets smarter with every message, every correction, every house completed.

## Build Order

### Phase 1: Prove It (current)
One superintendent opens the app every morning without being reminded.

> *"I checked Andon before I left the house this morning."*

That's the milestone. Nothing else matters until this is true.

### Phase 2: The API (build next)
Everything feeds into it. Everything reads from it.

Today: SMS + Voice + Email.
Tomorrow: Buildertrend, Procore, Zapier, ERP.
The engine stays the same. Nothing changes in the core architecture.

### Phase 3: Integrations (once Phase 1 is proven)
- Buildertrend schedule sync
- Zapier connector
- Multi-industry expansion
- AI enhancements (auto-assign, predictive Red)

---

## Product Principles

1. **Reduce uncertainty, don't store information.**
2. **Be the layer, not the platform.**
3. **The superintendent's morning routine is the only metric that matters.**
4. **Price on value (mistakes avoided, capacity gained), not features.**
5. **Everything goes through the same API. One engine, many inputs.**
6. **Don't let the vision outrun the evidence.**
